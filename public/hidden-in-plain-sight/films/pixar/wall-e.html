WALL-E (2008)

A nearly silent love story between two robots in a future where humanity has abandoned Earth

Praised by critics as Pixar's most "mature" film. Five things every parent should know:

1. EVE as wedding-dress Eve, WALL-E as Adam. EVE is designed egg-shaped, pure white, curved, and "mysterious." Her name is spoken repeatedly. WALL-E's attraction to her is built as a deliberate Genesis parallel — but the image-bearers of Yahuah have been replaced with robots. The creation account is rewritten as a machine evolution story, with two robots cast as humanity's spiritual heirs.

2. The plant as the "tree." A small green plant drives the entire plot, sealed inside EVE's belly and revealed at the climax. The imagery explicitly echoes Eden's Tree of Life — but here the "tree" is not forbidden, and salvation comes not through Yahushua but through restoring a houseplant. The redemption story of all humanity hinges on a single seedling.

3. No families exist on the Axiom. The space station shows adults, toddlers, and infants — but no mothers, fathers, or family groupings. Children are raised in institutional nursery pods. Marriage and biological family — the Torah's foundational unit (Genesis 2:24) — are simply absent from the human future presented as normal.

4. Earth-worship as the gospel. The driving theology of the film is that Earth is sacred, humanity has sinned against it through consumption, and salvation comes through ecological stewardship of the planet. This is the environmentalist gospel that substitutes worship of creation for worship of the Creator (Romans 1:25) — presented to children as unquestionable moral truth.

5. Buy N Large as one-world government. A single corporation caused Earth's ruin, runs the space colony, controls the media, feeds the population, and educates the children. The film trains young viewers to accept a one-world corporate-government system as the natural future of humanity — exactly the structure Revelation 13 warns against. Children are being normalized to a beast-system framework while sipping a juice box.
