SCI-FI & ACTION

The franchises Christians defend

Sci-fi and action franchises have a peculiar power that fairy tales do not. They feel modern, intelligent, even philosophical. Adults watch them. Pastors quote them from the pulpit. The merchandise sits on professionals' desks. Because they are not marketed as "magical," the spiritual content slips past the same parents who would refuse a Disney film for its witchcraft.

This is where the most sophisticated Eastern mysticism, gnostic theology, and counterfeit gospel narratives in modern entertainment are hidden. These films do not look like religion. They look like adventure. That is precisely what makes them effective.

"Beware lest any man spoil you through philosophy and vain deceit, after the tradition of men, after the rudiments of the world, and not after the Messiah." — Colossians 2:8
