**Zechariah 14:9**

**One Name in All the Earth**

*The day the idols fall and Yahuah alone is known*

When the earth knows Him at last, He is one Yahuah with one Name --- not
One made of three.

--- The Standing Stone ---

> **Behind "LORD" in your Bible lies a hidden name --- in the Hebrew it
> is Yahuah** *Psalm 83:18***; Yahuah is the Father** *Isaiah 63:16***;
> Yahuah is the only God, beside Him there is no other** *Isaiah
> 45:5***; therefore Yahuah the Father is the only true God, leaving no
> room for a second or third person** *1 Corinthians 8:6***.**

Part One

**The Trinitarian Claim**

***1.1 --- What Trinitarians Say About This Verse***

Zechariah 14:9 reads:

> **Zechariah 14:9**
>
> *And the LORD \[Yahuah\] shall be king over all the earth: in that day
> shall there be one LORD \[Yahuah\], and his name one.*

In Hebrew it reads: "bayyom hahu yihyeh Yahuah echad u'shmo echad."

Trinitarians use this verse in two ways. First, they say it describes
the return of Yahushua (Jesus) as Yahuah --- the LORD --- Himself, and
conclude from this that the Messiah is Yahuah. Second, they argue that
the Hebrew word echad does not simply mean "one." They claim it means a
"composite unity" --- more than one person joined together as one. This
is the same move they make on the Shema in *Deuteronomy 6:4*. So when
they read "Yahuah will be one," they hear "the multi-person God will at
last be shown in full."

***1.2 --- The Problems Before We Even Open the Text***

Before we examine a single Hebrew word, two things stand out. First,
notice what the verse actually says: Yahuah will be king. Not the Son.
Not a Trinity. Yahuah --- one name, one King.

Second, Paul tells us plainly how the story ends. In *1 Corinthians
15:24--28* the Son gives the kingdom back to the Father, and then the
Son Himself becomes subject to the Father, "that God may be all in all."
If the Son and the Father were co-equal, why would the Son hand the
kingdom over? Why would He be made subject to anyone? The Trinitarian
reading of Zechariah 14:9 collides with what Paul says about the end.

*Hebrews 1:1--2* adds another layer. Yahuah spoke "unto the fathers by
the prophets" in time past, but "in these last days" has spoken "by his
Son." The pattern is clear: in the Old Testament Yahuah spoke through
prophets and messengers, not through the Son. So when Zechariah writes
of Yahuah being king, there is no warrant for inserting the Son into the
picture.

Part Two

**What the Hebrew Actually Says**

***2.1 --- Echad Means "One" --- Plain and Simple***

**אֶחָד**

***echad***

*one --- the basic Hebrew numeral, used of single, undivided things*

Echad is the ordinary Hebrew number for "one." It appears thousands of
times across the Old Testament. It is the very word used in the Shema:
"Hear, O Israel: Yahuah our God, Yahuah is one." Zechariah 14:9 echoes
that confession almost word for word.

Trinitarians claim echad means "composite unity," but the word never
carries that meaning on its own. Watch how Scripture uses it. *Genesis
2:21* says Yahuah took "one of his ribs" --- not a composite rib.
*Ecclesiastes 4:8* speaks of "one alone, and there is not a second" ---
total singularity. *Deuteronomy 17:6* requires the testimony of more
than "one witness" --- a single person. *Genesis 42:13* says "one is
not" --- one individual, now gone. The word simply means one.

Here is the simplest way to see the problem. If echad always meant
"composite unity," then the Shema itself --- the most central confession
in all of Israel --- would be declaring that Yahuah is composite. That
is the exact opposite of what the Shema was given to say.

***2.2 --- Zechariah Echoes the Shema***

Readers have long noticed that Zechariah 14:9 is built as a deliberate
echo of the Shema in *Deuteronomy 6:4*. The point is not that Yahuah
turns into a different kind of "one." The point is that His kingship
becomes known everywhere. On that day no one will pray to Baal or bow to
Ashteroth. The confession "Yahuah is one" will be the reality of the
whole earth, not Israel alone.

***2.3 --- The Septuagint Agrees***

The Septuagint is the ancient Greek translation of the Hebrew
Scriptures, completed centuries before Yahushua was born. It renders
Zechariah 14:9 in a simple, straightforward way: "The Lord will be one,
and His name one." No hint of plurality. No composite deity. The Greek
reads the verse exactly as the plain Hebrew does --- one Yahuah,
worshipped alone, with no rival.

***2.4 --- Major Translations Confirm the Plain Reading***

If there were any real ambiguity in the Hebrew, the major translations
would show it. They do not. The KJV reads, "in that day shall there be
one LORD, and his name one." The NASB reads, "The LORD will be the only
one, and His name the only one." The NLT reads, "there will be one LORD
--- his name alone will be worshiped." Across the board the verse is
read the same way: this is about exclusive worship --- not about what
Yahuah is made of on the inside.

Part Three

**The Context Trinitarians Ignore**

***3.1 --- Zechariah 13--14 Is About Destroying Idols***

The surest way to read any verse is to read what comes before it. Just
one chapter earlier, Zechariah 13:2 says:

> **Zechariah 13:2**
>
> *And it shall come to pass in that day, saith the LORD \[Yahuah\] of
> hosts, that I will cut off the names of the idols out of the land, and
> they shall no more be remembered.*

Mark the phrase "cut off the names of the idols." Now read 14:9 again:
"his name one." The link is unmistakable. When the idols are removed and
their names forgotten, only one name remains --- the name of Yahuah.
That is what "his name one" means. It says nothing about how many
persons Yahuah contains. It is about wiping out every false god.

***3.2 --- "His Name One" --- No More Rival Gods***

All through the Old Testament, Yahuah's "name" is bound up with His
demand for exclusive worship. *Hosea 2:17* promises to take away "the
names of Baalim out of her mouth," to be remembered no more. *Exodus
23:13* commands, "make no mention of the name of other gods." *Isaiah
42:8* declares, "I am Yahuah: that is my name: and my glory will I not
give to another." *Psalm 83:18* prays that men may know "that thou,
whose name alone is Yahuah, art the most high over all the earth."

When Zechariah says "his name one," he means that no other god's name
will ever be spoken in worship again. The Baals are gone. The Ashteroth
are gone. Every false god is gone. Only the name of Yahuah will be
called upon. This is the end of false religion --- not a description of
what Yahuah looks like on the inside.

***3.3 --- The Prophets All Paint the Same Picture***

Zechariah 14:9 does not stand alone. The same vision returns again and
again in the prophets. *Isaiah 2:17--18* says "the LORD alone shall be
exalted in that day," and the idols utterly abolished. *Isaiah 44:6*
says, "I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no
God." *Isaiah 45:5* says, "I am the LORD, and there is none else, there
is no God beside me." *Deuteronomy 4:35* says, "the LORD he is God;
there is none else beside him."

Every one of these points to the same day: when the whole world knows
that Yahuah alone is God. Not one of them describes a multi-person God.
All of them describe the end of idol worship.

Part Four

**The Witness of Yahushua and Paul**

***4.1 --- Yahushua Affirms the Shema Without Changing a Word***

In *Mark 12:29* someone asks Yahushua which commandment matters most. He
answers, "Hear, O Israel; The Lord our God is one Lord." He quotes the
Shema exactly. He does not add "in three persons." He does not explain
that "one" really means "three-in-one." He simply says it.

The scribe answers in verse 32, "there is one God; and there is none
other but he." And in verse 34 Yahushua tells him, "Thou art not far
from the kingdom of God." Yahushua does not correct the man --- He
commends him. The scribe said God is one and there is no other, and
Yahushua said that answer puts a person near the kingdom. That is the
very theology of Zechariah 14:9.

***4.2 --- Yahushua Calls the Father the Only True God***

In *John 17:3* Yahushua prays, "And this is life eternal, that they
might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast
sent." Notice two things. The Father is named "the only true God." And
Yahushua sets Himself apart as the one sent by that God. He does not
claim to be the only true God; He says the Father is.

***4.3 --- Paul Tells Us How the Story Ends***

*1 Corinthians 15:24--28* describes the very end of the story:

> **1 Corinthians 15:24, 28**
>
> *Then cometh the end, when he shall have delivered up the kingdom to
> God, even the Father... And when all things shall be subdued unto him,
> then shall the Son also himself be subject unto him that put all
> things under him, that God may be all in all.*

Three things leap off the page. First, the Messiah delivers the kingdom
up to the Father; He does not keep it for Himself. Second, the Son
becomes subject to the Father --- and you cannot be made subject to one
who is your equal. Third, the whole purpose is "that God may be all in
all" --- not a Trinity, but God the Father, ruling over everything, the
Son included. This is exactly what Zechariah 14:9 looks like when it is
fulfilled: Yahuah the Father, sole King over all the earth.

***4.4 --- Paul Defines Who the "One God" Is***

In *1 Corinthians 8:6* Paul writes:

> **1 Corinthians 8:6**
>
> *But to us there is but one God, the Father, of whom are all things,
> and we in him; and one Lord Jesus Christ, by whom are all things, and
> we by him.*

Read it carefully. Paul says the "one God" is the Father. Yahushua is
the Lord --- the agent through whom the Father works. But the Messiah is
not the one God. Paul drew a clear line, and the New Testament never
erases it.

Conclusion

**The Verdict**

Zechariah 14:9 is one of the clearest statements of singular worship in
the Hebrew Bible. It does not describe a composite God finally pulling
Himself together. It describes a broken world finally bowing to the one
God it should have worshipped all along.

Every false name will be silenced. Every idol will be smashed. And every
nation on earth will say what Israel has confessed since Sinai: "Hear, O
Israel: Yahuah our God, Yahuah is one."

> **If "Yahuah will be one" means a Trinity will be revealed, then
> "Yahuah is one" in the Shema means Israel confessed a Trinity at Sinai
> --- and no Trinitarian will say that, because Israel never believed
> it.**
